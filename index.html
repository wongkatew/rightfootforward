Conditional Independence and D-Separation in Bayes Nets Exploration

UC San Diego | November 2018 - December 2018

This is a learning tool that lets students explore the relationships in conditional independence and d-separation in Bayes Nets by examining different graphs and nodes. The user selects nodes in a Bayes Net that will be checked for whether or not they are conditionally independent and why or why not. We also provide practice problems that allow the user to test their knowledge by submitting answers and seeing whether or not their reasoning makes sense.

This was a three-week project in a team of 2 for UCSD CSE 150: Introduction to AI, Probabilistic Reasoning and Decision Making.
